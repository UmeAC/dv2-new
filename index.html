Chapter 02

Daytrip Spending Through the Pandemic Years

The heatmap captures every state and every quarter from 2019 to 2025 — revealing the 2020 collapse, the 2021 domestic bloom, and the uneven return to pre-pandemic peaks.

Quarterly Daytrip Expenditure by State ($M) — 2019 to 2025
Each cell = state × quarter. Darker purple = higher spending. Hover for exact figures.
Key pattern: The near-white strip from March–September 2020 marks the lockdown collapse. By late 2021, NSW and VIC had roared back — even exceeding 2019 baselines — as Australians redirected cancelled overseas travel budgets domestically.

The Pent-Up Demand Dividend

Between 2021 and 2023, domestic travel didn't just recover — in several states it surged well past pre-pandemic levels. Australians redirected spending from cancelled overseas holidays into road trips, regional breaks, and weekend getaways. This "stay-at-home tourism" boom reshaped regional economies.

Drag to brush the top chart and zoom the spend chart below. See how arrivals and spend track together across two decades.
